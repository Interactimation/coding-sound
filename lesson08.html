Sound Synthesis 02: LFO

An LFO or Low Frequency Oscillator is like oscillators we've used before but they're for modulating signals, rather than for creating listenable tones —in fact the frequencies used are generally below 20 Hz, just outside the range of human hearing

  • The LFO signal is not producing audio but is plugged into —for instance— the amplitude (or "volume") signal of a different oscillator, or its frequency signal
    • The resulting wave applied to volume would raise and lower amplitude in time with the cycling of the LFO
    • Applied to the frequency, the waved of the LFO might produce a sense of vibrato
